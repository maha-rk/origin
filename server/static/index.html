ORIGIN

ORIGIN investigates environmental and land-use claims — deforestation, protected-area encroachment, water risk, carbon offset legitimacy, and more — by checking them against real satellite, ecological, and climate data instead of taking them at their word. Every verdict traces back to the evidence behind it, so you can see exactly why, not just what.

How it works

  1. 1

    Understand the claim

    Location Grounding resolves the claim's location to coordinates, while Claim Decomposition splits compound assertions into independently checkable parts — run concurrently, not one after the other.

  2. 2

    Gather evidence in parallel

    Seven agents fan out at once across real registries: tree-cover loss (Global Forest Watch), protected areas (WDPA), water and disaster risk (GDACS), satellite visual inspection (Gemini), vegetation trend (Earth Engine NDVI), carbon project registries (Carbonmark), and long-run climate trend (NASA POWER).

  3. 3

    Cross-reference and decide

    Cross-Reference weighs every finding against the claim, then Verdict Synthesis produces a direction score, an evidence-coverage score, and a written verdict with full source citations — never a bare opinion.

How to use it

  1. Describe a claim about a specific place — a coordinate pair, or a named location.
  2. Watch each agent report back live as it resolves the location and checks real data sources.
  3. Read the verdict: contradicting vs. supporting evidence, an evidence-coverage score, and the sources behind every finding.
